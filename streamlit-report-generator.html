The Report Generator Story
A Journey of Collaboration

Sometimes the best solutions come from the simplest conversations. This is the story of how a casual coffee chat transformed into our most-used tool at Marex - and taught me that the best technology is the kind that disappears, leaving only empowered people behind.

The Beginning

It was a Thursday morning, and Tom from operations looked exhausted. "I wish I could just upload this file and get my report," he said, gesturing at his screen full of Excel formulas. The frustration in his voice was palpable - he'd been wrestling with the same reconciliation process for hours.

"What if you could?" I asked, genuinely curious.

His eyes lit up. "Is that even possible?"

That simple exchange sparked what became our most transformative project. Tom wasn't asking for complex technology - he was asking for his time back. He wanted to focus on analysis, not data manipulation. That conversation changed everything.

The Journey

The next day, I knocked on Tom's door. "Show me exactly what you do," I said, pulling up a chair beside him. For the next hour, we went through his entire process step by step. I asked questions he'd never considered:

  • "What happens when this field is empty?"
  • "How do you handle duplicate entries?"
  • "What if the file format changes?"
  • "Who else needs reports like this?"

Tom's answers revealed something beautiful: he wasn't just doing data entry - he was applying years of business logic and institutional knowledge. My job wasn't to replace his expertise, but to amplify it.

That weekend, I built the first prototype. Nothing fancy - just a simple Streamlit app that could take his Excel file and spit out a basic report. Monday morning, I showed it to Tom.

"It works!" he exclaimed, then paused. "But what about when we need to handle the month-end adjustments?"

And so began our iterative journey. Each week brought new insights:

Week 1: Basic file upload and processing
Week 2: Added reconciliation logic Tom suggested
Week 3: Sarah from accounting joined our sessions - "Can it handle multiple currencies?"
Week 4: Mike from compliance asked about audit trails
Week 5: The tool was handling edge cases I never would have thought of alone

Each conversation made the solution better. This wasn't my project anymore - it was ours.

The Solution

What emerged wasn't just a reporting tool - it was a collaboration platform disguised as software. The final application reflected months of conversations, iterations, and shared problem-solving.

The Magic of Simplicity

The interface you see below represents countless hours of "What if we moved this here?" and "Could we make this simpler?" The drag-and-drop functionality wasn't my idea - it came from watching Tom struggle with file uploads. The real-time preview feature emerged from Sarah's comment: "I wish I could see what's happening."

Every feature tells a story of collaboration:

  • Drag-and-Drop Upload: "Tom, what if you could just drag the file here?"
  • Real-time Validation: "Sarah, what if the system told you about problems immediately?"
  • Multiple Output Formats: "Mike, what if you could get the same data as a PDF for compliance and Excel for analysis?"
  • Audit Trail: "What if we tracked every change automatically?"

The Technology Behind the Magic

While the interface looks simple, the underlying technology is sophisticated:

  • Streamlit: Chosen for rapid prototyping and user feedback
  • Pandas: Handles complex data transformations Tom's team needed
  • Plotly: Creates the visualizations Sarah requested
  • Custom Validation Logic: Implements the business rules we discovered together

But the real technology was the conversation. Every line of code represents a question asked, a problem understood, and a solution crafted together.

The Impact

Six months later, I walked past Tom's desk. He was leaning back in his chair, coffee in hand, reviewing an analysis report. "Remember when you used to spend your mornings fighting with Excel?" I asked.

He laughed. "I can't believe I used to do that manually. Now I actually get to think about what the data means instead of just collecting it."

That's when I knew we'd succeeded. The technology had disappeared, leaving only empowered people behind.

The Numbers Tell a Story

But the real impact goes beyond metrics:

  • 200+ active users across 15 departments - each with their own story
  • 1,000+ reports monthly - representing thousands of hours returned to meaningful work
  • 95% user satisfaction - but more importantly, genuine enthusiasm
  • Zero IT support tickets for report generation - the system just works

Sarah from accounting told me: "I used to dread month-end reporting. Now I look forward to it because I can focus on the insights instead of the mechanics."

Mike from compliance said: "The audit trail feature you built saves me hours of documentation. It's like having a perfect memory of every decision."

The Lessons

This project taught me that citizen development isn't about the technology - it's about the relationships. The best solutions emerge from genuine collaboration, not isolated coding sessions.

What I Learned About People

  • Everyone is an expert in their own domain - my job is to listen and amplify their expertise
  • The best features come from watching people work, not from technical specifications
  • User adoption happens when people feel heard and valued in the development process
  • Iteration is everything - the first version is never the final version

What I Learned About Technology

The most powerful technology is invisible. When Tom forgets he's using software and just focuses on his analysis, that's when I know I've succeeded. The goal isn't to impress people with clever code - it's to solve their problems so elegantly they forget the problem ever existed.

What's Next

This tool continues to evolve because the conversations continue. Just last week, someone asked, "What if we could share report templates across teams?" And so the journey continues, one conversation at a time.

The best part of being a citizen developer? Every day brings a new puzzle to solve together.
