Career Progression

My evolution from futures brokerage analyst to citizen developer happened organically. It started with a simple need: automating my own repetitive tasks to focus on more strategic work. But when colleagues saw the results, they began approaching me with their own challenges.

This organic growth demonstrated something powerful about citizen development in large enterprises. When the head of clearing needed interest calculations and IT estimated over a year for delivery, I was able to provide a working Python script in hours. That speed advantage - delivering solutions in hours vs. years - became a defining characteristic of my approach.

What makes this approach successful isn't replacing formal IT processes, but complementing them. I work directly with our data team, collaborate with IT governance, and ensure solutions meet enterprise standards while maintaining the agility that business users desperately need.

What Drives My Passion

There's something incredibly satisfying about sitting down with a team, understanding their real problems, and knowing that together we can figure out a solution. I believe the most important thing isn't having all the technical answers - it's understanding the business problem deeply enough that the right solution reveals itself.

Every manual process I encounter is an opportunity. Every frustrated colleague represents a chance to give someone their time back. When I see a team spending hours on tasks that could be automated in minutes, I see potential - potential to free people to focus on work that truly matters.

My technical skills opened doors, but my attitude keeps me solving problems. I don't quit. I don't give up. Because in every challenge, there's an opportunity to learn something incredible and help someone work more effectively.

Continuous Learning

This isn't just academic curiosity - it's about staying ahead of the curve to provide even better solutions for enterprise challenges.
