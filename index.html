The Interest Calculator Story

The Beginning: It was a Tuesday afternoon when the clearing team burst into my corner of the office. "John, we have a problem," they said. The firm had no way to calculate interest on our options EOD and ITD, and clients were waiting.

The Journey: I pulled up a chair and said, "Tell me everything." What started as a complex financial calculation challenge became a collaborative puzzle-solving session. That evening, I stayed late and developed a quick Python script - nothing fancy, but it worked.

The Impact: Today, what once caused daily stress runs automatically. The clearing team can focus on strategic work instead of manual calculations. But more importantly, we prevented potential losses and ensured our clients receive accurate interest payments.

"This project taught me that the best solutions come from true collaboration. As a citizen developer, I'm not just writing code - I'm translating human needs into digital solutions."

The Data Liberation Story

Where It All Began

The Foundation: Every journey has a beginning. This academic project was where I first discovered my love for turning messy data into meaningful insights. Working with a massive movie dataset, I learned that data cleaning isn't just about code - it's about understanding the story the data wants to tell.

The Learning: Through SQL and SAS, I discovered that the most beautiful visualizations mean nothing if the underlying data isn't clean. This project taught me patience, attention to detail, and the importance of questioning every assumption.

The Connection: Looking back, this is where my passion for helping others with data challenges first sparked. Every manual process I see at Marex reminds me of those late nights cleaning movie data - and how much better it feels when the process is automated.

"This project planted the seed that grew into my mission: making data work for people, not the other way around."

Living Two Lives at Marex

Being both a Futures Brokerage Analyst and a Citizen Developer isn't about wearing two hats - it's about building bridges.

"Every problem is an opportunity to help. Every solution is a chance to give someone their time back."
